## 1. Situation

**a. Area of Operations.** Pisgah Ranger District, Pisgah National Forest — Transylvania/Haywood/Henderson counties, NC. Base of operations: Brevard, NC (Intermediate Staging Base — ISB). Objective area per Annex A: US-276 corridor north past the Sycamore Cove and Cat Gap/John Rock trailheads, past Cradle of Forestry, turning onto Yellow Gap Road (FS 1206) just past Pink Beds Loop Trail (first right), then north on Yellow Gap Rd to the Patrol Base vicinity (Wolf Ford), short of the Limit of Advance (see para 3b, Phase II).

**b. Terrain/Weather.** Mountainous, forested, narrow gravel forest roads. Late June: afternoon thunderstorm risk, warm days, cool nights at elevation. Cell coverage reliable in Brevard / US-276 corridor, unreliable to absent on Yellow Gap Rd. Sections of the district still carry Hurricane Helene damage — the road beyond Wolf Ford is closed for that reason.

**c. Friendly Forces.** None — single-operator patrol, no coordination required.

**d. Civil Considerations.** Forest Service staff at the Ranger Station — **(828) 866-3437**, 1600 Pisgah Highway, Pisgah Forest, NC 28768. Day hikers, anglers, and equestrian traffic on Yellow Gap Rd / Wolf Ford Horse Camp; other campers at the facility. PB1 confirmed as **Wolf Ford Horse Campground proper, Site A9** (not dispersed camping nearby) — reservation placed and confirmed. Gated access; the reservation confirmation lists **two codes, both labeled "Entrance Combination Lock Code": 1111 and 2222** — likely either separate codes for two different gates/locks, or one current code plus one stale from a prior printing. **Confirm which applies at the OBJ RANGER stop or by calling the camp host/reservation line before you're standing at the gate with no signal.** Carry both, written down, in the gear list (see para 4e and Annex C) — not just on the phone.

**e. Fire Restriction.** All open burning and campfires are prohibited across Pisgah, Nantahala, Uwharrie, and Croatan National Forests outside developed recreation sites — in effect since 15 APR 2026, no rescission posted, fire danger rated Extreme. Stove is the default for this entire patrol. Wolf Ford's campground alert permits attended charcoal/gas grills for cooking purposes only (wood-burning open fires remain prohibited); bring one as a backup if you want, and do a quick visual check of Site A9's fire ring on arrival, but plan around the stove regardless.

## 2. Mission

Operator conducts a 4-day/3-night movement-to-bivouac and recon patrol from Brevard into the Pisgah Ranger District, 29 JUN–02 JUL 2026, **in order to** confirm current road/closure status, scout designated waterfall and trail objectives, establish and strike a dispersed camp, complete one POTA activation, and validate the radio/comms plan — returning all personnel and equipment to home station NLT 02 JUL.

## 3. Execution

**a. Commander's Intent.**
- *Purpose:* shake out gear, comms, and nav SOPs in a real field environment, low stakes, before anything bigger.
- *Key tasks:* (1) confirm Yellow Gap Rd status at the source — ranger station — before committing; (2) respect the Limit of Advance at the gate east of Wolf Ford; (3) actually get the radios programmed and tested before relying on them; (4) log a clean POTA activation; (5) leave no trace; (6) maintain fire discipline — stove only, no open flame, attended grill only as a backup.
- *End state:* operator and vehicle back at home station, gear serviceable, no incidents, one POTA log in the book, tank full again.

**b. Concept of Operations.** Four phases, keyed to the four days. The patrol itself runs as one continuous movement bracketed by a Start Point and Release Point — both the Shell Station, US 64/US 276 junction, Pisgah Forest (20 Hendersonville Hwy) — from Tuesday morning to Wednesday afternoon. PB1 (Wolf Ford) is an overnight halt inside that bracket, not the patrol's endpoint; the patrol doesn't close out until RP.

### Phase I — Movement & Recon Prep (Mon 29 JUN)
- Drive in from home station, check into the ISB (hotel, Brevard).
- **OBJ RECON** — explore Brevard, square away last-minute logistics (fuel, ice, supplies).
- **OBJ NETCALL** (evening) — identify active 2m/70cm/GMRS repeaters in range and program the FT-65R and UV-9G. Working list at para 5b — re-verify in RepeaterBook/myGMRS before locking in channel memories; repeater status drifts over time.
- If ranger station hours allow, swing by for an early closure check and to confirm the Wolf Ford gate code; otherwise this rolls into Phase II.
- Dinner, rest.

### Phase II — Insertion & Patrol Base (Tue 30 JUN) — mounted/dismounted patrol, see Annex B for full leg detail
- **Mounted:** depart ISB — **SP** at the Shell Station (US 64/US 276 junction, Pisgah Forest; grid ref TBD — capture via GPS on first pass through) — continue north on US-276.
- **OBJ RANGER** (42950 06001 / 35.2848615, -82.7269817) — Pisgah Ranger Station. Confirm Yellow Gap Rd / FS 1206 status before committing further, confirm which Wolf Ford gate code (1111 or 2222) currently applies if possible, and ask about Cat Gap/John Rock trailhead and parking access — the adjacent Bobby N. Setzer Fish Hatchery is mid-renovation (multi-year project beginning 2026) and access status for this trip's dates hasn't been independently confirmed.
- **Dismount, OBJ CASCADE** — Looking Glass Falls (39164 07307 / 35.2960335, -82.7688443) and Moore Cove Falls, short roadside stops on US-276.
- **Mounted**, pass the Cat Gap/John Rock trailhead without dismounting (that hike is Wednesday's OBJ CAT, on the way back) — continue north on US-276 past Cradle of Forestry, turn onto Yellow Gap Road (FS 1206) just past Pink Beds Loop Trail (first right), then north on Yellow Gap Rd to the Patrol Base vicinity (Wolf Ford).
- Arrive Wolf Ford Horse Campground (PB1, 41756 16719 / 35.3812717, -82.7421715), **Site A9** — gated entry. Have the confirmed gate code on hand at the gate, not just saved on a phone that may be dead or signal-locked.
- **LIMIT OF ADVANCE — gate on Yellow Gap Road just east of the Wolf Ford Rd (FS 5176) junction, confirmed at 41821 16546 (35.379721, -82.741431). Vehicle does not pass this gate.** Matches the official FS closure boundary (open Hwy 276/Cradle of Forestry to Wolf Ford; closed Wolf Ford to North Mills River Campground).
- **OBJ ACTIVATE (primary)** — POTA activation, Pisgah National Forest, reference **K-4510 / US-4510**, run right after camp is up — full daylight, fresh, gear already off the vehicle. Check on-site whether the camp also falls inside Pisgah Game Land (K-6937 / US-6937) — well-precedented two-fer for this general area; worth the quick boundary check once on site. **If camp setup runs long and there isn't time before OBJ PILOT, skip this slot — fall back to the evening window below.**
- **Dismount, OBJ PILOT** — road-walk from the gate to the Pilot Cove/Slate Rock trailhead (~1.8 mi each way) and complete the **short loop only** (~3.6 mi, 931 ft gain) — not the longer combined Pilot Cove–Slate Rock loop (6.8–6.9 mi), which isn't worth it this trip given the road walk already adds distance to a full hiking day. The road-walk distance is Annex B's least-certain figure (one source puts it at 1.8 mi past a closure point) — **set a hard clock-based turnaround, not a distance-based one**, so the uncertainty can't eat into the evening. Note: Pilot Rock Trail #321 (foot-only, 3.6 mi one way via Thompson Creek, ~1,800 ft gain, rated Difficult, no vehicle access) continues north from this area but is outside this leg's scope.
- **OBJ ACTIVATE (fallback)** — only if the primary slot above didn't happen. Run in the evening once back at camp — can run through the 1800–2000 check-in window since the gear's already up.

### Phase III — Patrol & Extraction (Wed 1 JUL) — mounted/dismounted, see Annex B for full leg detail
- Break camp. Police the site — pack out all trash, including food scraps.
- **Mounted:** road march from Patrol Base back down Yellow Gap Rd to US-276, then south.
- **Dismount, OBJ CAT** — Cat Gap Loop / John Rock, at the same trailhead passed Tuesday. Primary route is the full loop (5.9 mi, ~1,100 ft gain, 3–3.5 hr); the Cat Gap Bypass (4.8 mi, 2.5–3 hr) is the lever to pull if the morning ran long and you want a tighter margin before the evening check-in.
- **OBJ ACTIVATE (2nd)** — second POTA activation, same reference, Pisgah National Forest **K-4510 / US-4510**, run from the Cat Gap/John Rock TH parking area right after the hike, before driving out. This is a second activation of the same park, not a new two-fer like Tuesday's — still a valid, separately-logged activation. **Contingent on the trailhead access check at OBJ RANGER Tuesday morning** (para 3b, Phase II) — don't plan on this slot until that's confirmed.
- **Mounted:** continue south — **RP** at the Shell Station (US 64/US 276 junction, same as SP) — arrive ISB (hotel, Brevard). Patrol closes out at RP.
- Clean up, dinner, stand down.

### Phase IV — Return to Home Station (Thu 2 JUL)
- Final gear check, fuel up, depart ISB for home station.

**c. Tasks (standing, all phases).**
- Full tank before leaving pavement; recovery strap + shovel staged and accessible.
- Food/scented items locked in vehicle overnight — bear country.
- No confirmed potable water at Wolf Ford (remote horse camp, pit toilets) — carry what's needed regardless.
- Camp at the reserved site, Site A9, within Wolf Ford Horse Campground. If camping anywhere else on this trip ends up being dispersed instead, that's marked-numbered-roadside-site rules, not "anywhere within 1000 ft of the road."
- No open wood fire anywhere this trip. Stove is primary; an attended charcoal/gas grill is permitted at Wolf Ford for cooking only, per campground rules, if used as a backup.

**d. Coordinating Instructions.**
- **Declination: 9.0°W (9°02'), per WMM2025.** Re-zero compasses/maps to this value before taking a real bearing.
- Map scale 1:25,000. GPS app is primary nav; paper/compass is backup only.
- **Check-in windows:** 0600–0800 and 1800–2000 Eastern, daily, 29 JUN–2 JUL. One text per window, one line of status (e.g. *"heading out, ETA camp ~2-3PM"* / *"at camp, all good"* / *"breaking camp, heading back"* / *"back at hotel"*). Primary method: cell text. Fallback if no cell signal: Starlink at camp for data/voice (same Emergency-tier asset from para 5b PACE, doing double duty as the routine check-in fallback). A heads-up text sent *before* a window — *"running behind, expect my text closer to 8"* — doesn't count as a miss; it resets the clock, not the alarm. Mon/Thu windows are low-stakes since you're in full cell coverage; **Tue evening ("at camp") and Wed morning ("breaking camp") are the two that actually matter** — those are the ones most likely to ride on Starlink instead of cell.
- Check weather before Tuesday departure — afternoon storm risk is the main variable.

## 5. Command and Signal

**a. Command.**
- Single-element patrol — no succession required.
- Before losing signal Tuesday, give HERON 1 the route (SP at the Shell Station → US-276 north past Cradle of Forestry → Yellow Gap Rd/FS 1206 north → Patrol Base short of the gate east of Wolf Ford) and your expected return window. HERON 1 is your trigger for escalation if you go dark past it.
- **Trusted contact: HERON 1.** Mission packet (route, schedule, grid references, check-in windows) held by HERON 1 (Rear-D); a duplicate carried in the field per Annex C; a third copy in the vehicle glovebox.
- **Check-in method:** text message, 2x daily, within the windows defined in para 3d.
- **Escalation:**
  1. **1 missed window** → HERON 1 tries to make contact ASAP on every channel available.
  2. **2 consecutive missed windows** (~12 hrs of silence) → HERON 1 calls Pisgah Ranger Station, **(828) 866-3437**, to flag you as overdue and ask if they've seen or heard anything.
  3. **3 consecutive missed windows** (~24 hrs of silence) → HERON 1 calls 911 / local SAR with the route and grid references below, using the emergency callout format.
  4. **Unconditional backstop:** regardless of window count, no contact by the NLT 02 JUL return time is an automatic call — no need to count misses by then.

**Emergency callout format — read this, don't improvise it under stress:**

> "U.S. National Grid, one-seven Sierra Lima Victor, [easting], [northing]. Backup lat/long: [XX]° [XX.XX]' North, [XX]° [XX.XX]' West."

State the full grid reference every time — zone and 100km square included, not just the digits. The SAR team will work in USNG natively; the lat/long is there only in case the 911 call-taker's system doesn't recognize USNG (adoption is locally inconsistent — give it in degrees + decimal minutes, the land-SAR convention, not decimal degrees).

**Licensing notes** (so Monday night's programming session doesn't hit a wall):
- FT-65R is amateur-only (Part 97) — transmitting needs a Technician class license or higher.
- UV-9G on GMRS needs a GMRS license (no test, $35 for 10 years, covers immediate family). Worth knowing: Baofeng-style radios like the UV-9G aren't FCC type-accepted for GMRS even though they're commonly run that way in the field.
- G90 (HF) needs at least Technician (limited HF privileges) or General, depending on band/mode, for the POTA activation.
