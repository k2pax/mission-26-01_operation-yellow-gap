# Annex C to OPORD 26-01, YELLOW GAP — Personal & Vehicle Load-Out

**Classification:** UNCLASSIFIED // PERSONAL USE ONLY

Built against the trip's known constraints: 4 days/3 nights, bear country, **no potable water at Wolf Ford**, **stove only — open-fire restriction active since 15 APR 2026, no end date set**, single-operator with no backup, HF/VHF/UHF/GMRS comms suite, one POTA activation.

---

## 1. Personal Load-Out

### Worn / EDC
- [ ] Boots, broken in
- [ ] Moisture-wicking base layers (warm June days)
- [ ] Insulating layer for cool nights at elevation
- [ ] Rain shell — afternoon storm risk is the OPORD's stated main weather variable
- [ ] Hat (sun) + separate warm hat for evenings
- [ ] Sunglasses, sunscreen, insect repellent
- [ ] Knife / multitool
- [ ] Watch (for hitting the comm windows without relying on a phone)

### Navigation
- [ ] Compass — re-zero to **9.0°W** (per WMM2025)
- [ ] Paper topo, USGS 1:25,000 (Annex A) — backup only, per OPORD
- [ ] GPS app loaded with offline maps for the AO, phone fully charged + power bank

### Communications
- [ ] Cell phone + cable/power bank
- [ ] FT-65R (VHF/UHF amateur) + spare battery, charged
- [ ] UV-9G (GMRS) + spare battery, charged
- [ ] G90 (HF) + power source for POTA activation, charged
- [ ] Antenna(s) and supports for the activation
- [ ] **Printed copy of the mission packet** (route, schedule, contacts, check-in windows, grid refs) — a duplicate of the master copy held by HERON 1 (Rear-D)
- [ ] **Printed reservation confirmation for Site A9, with both gate codes (1111 and 2222)** — confirm which applies at the Ranger Station stop or by phone before relying on either, and carry both written down regardless, not just on the phone
- [ ] 9-Line MEDEVAC card (Annex E) and SALUTE report card (Annex D)
- [ ] Copy of Technician-class and GMRS license info

### Shelter & Sleep
- [ ] Tent + stakes/guylines (gravel/grass tent pads at Wolf Ford — bring options for both)
- [ ] Sleeping bag rated for cool mountain nights
- [ ] Sleeping pad
- [ ] Ground tarp/footprint

### Cook & Water — stove only, no open fire
- [ ] Stove + **fuel with margin** (no campfire backup this trip per fire restriction)
- [ ] Lighter + backup ignition, stored dry
- [ ] Pot/mug, spork
- [ ] **All drinking/cooking water for the stay** — Wolf Ford has no potable source. Calculate ~1 gal/day minimum × 2 nights at camp + reserve.
- [ ] Water filter/treatment as a backup only, not primary plan
- [ ] (Optional) Charcoal/gas grill setup as a stove backup — attended use is permitted at Wolf Ford for cooking only; still do a quick visual check of Site A9's fire ring on arrival

### Food & Bear Safety
- [ ] Food for all meals, repackaged to minimize bulk/scent
- [ ] Bear-resistant storage (canister or hard-sided container — vehicle storage overnight per OPORD para 4a)
- [ ] Trash bags — pack out everything, including food scraps

### Medical
- [ ] Personal first aid kit
- [ ] Personal medications
- [ ] Emergency/space blanket
- [ ] Note of nearest hospital (Transylvania Regional, Brevard) in mission packet

### Documents & Money
- [ ] ID
- [ ] Cash (no card readers at a remote horse camp)
- [ ] Insurance/medical info card

### Tools & Misc
- [ ] Headlamp + spare batteries
- [ ] Paracord, repair tape
- [ ] Trowel (pit toilets at Wolf Ford — bring your own TP, hand sanitizer)
- [ ] Biodegradable soap

### Comms
